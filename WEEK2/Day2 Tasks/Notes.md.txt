SECTION 6 — THE COMPLETE O2C CYCLE
Step	T-Code	Purpose	Module
1	VA01	Create Sales Order	SD
2	VL01N	Create Delivery	SD/MM
3	VL02N	Post Goods Issue (601)	SD/MM/FI
4	VF01	Create Billing	SD/FI
5	F-28	Customer Payment	FI
Step 1 – Sales Order (VA01)
	• Customer commits to buy.
	• No inventory changes.
	• No accounting entries.
Step 2 – Delivery (VL01N)
	• Delivery document is created.
	• Stock availability is checked.
	• Warehouse prepares shipment.
	• Inventory still does not decrease.
Step 3 – Goods Issue (VL02N)
Movement Type: 601
This is the most important step.
Effects:
	• Inventory decreases.
	• Goods leave the warehouse.
	• Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) is posted.
	• Accounting entries are created.
Answer to your key question: Inventory decreases during Goods Issue (VL02N) using Movement Type 601.
Step 4 – Billing (VF01)
	• Invoice is generated.
	• Customer Accounts Receivable (AR) increases.
	• Revenue is recognized.
Step 5 – Payment Receipt (F-28)
	• Customer pays.
	• Bank account increases.
	• Accounts Receivable is cleared.

SECTION 8 — SAP BASIS FOCUS
Although Basis engineers don't create sales orders, they support the system.
Daily tasks:
	• Monitor application servers.
	• Check work processes.
	• Resolve user login issues.
	• Monitor background jobs.
	• Ensure printers can print invoices.
	• Monitor system performance during month-end billing.
Production issue example:
	• Billing jobs fail because the filesystem is full.
	• Basis clears space and restarts the job.

SECTION 12 — TROUBLESHOOTING
Scenario 1: Sales Order Cannot Be Created
Symptoms:
	• User gets an authorization error.
Investigation:
	• Check user roles.
	• Check system logs.
Resolution:
	• Assign the correct authorization.
Scenario 2: Billing Fails
Symptoms:
	• Invoice not generated.
Root Cause:
	• Pricing or configuration issue.
Resolution:
	• SD team fixes pricing; Basis ensures system availability.
Scenario 3: Goods Issue Fails
Symptoms:
	• Warehouse cannot post Goods Issue.
Root Cause:
	• Insufficient stock.
Resolution:
	• Update inventory or wait for replenishment.

Detailed Steps 

Order-to-Cash (O2C) Process in SAP (Detailed)
Think of a car manufacturer like Honda selling 100 motorcycles to a dealer.
Business Flow: Customer Order ? Delivery ? Goods Issue ? Billing ? Payment

Step 1: Sales Order
Goal
	• Record the customer's order.
	• Confirm what the customer wants to buy.
Business Activity The customer commits to purchase products.
SAP Module
	• SD (Sales and Distribution)
Main T-Code
	• VA01 – Create Sales Order
	• (Display: VA03, Change: VA02)
What Happens in SAP?
	• Customer is selected.
	• Material is selected.
	• Quantity and price are entered.
	• Delivery date is determined.
	• Sales Order document is created.
Business Example A Honda dealer orders 100 motorcycles.
Database Impact
	• Sales Order record is created.
	• No inventory changes.
	• No accounting entries.
SAP Basis Role
	• Ensure users can log in.
	• Check authorizations for VA01.
	• Monitor application performance.
SAP Cloud/DevOps Role
	• Keep application servers available.
	• Monitor CPU, memory, storage, and network.
	• Ensure backups and high availability.

Step 2: Delivery Creation
Goal
	• Prepare the goods for shipment.
Business Activity Warehouse receives instructions to ship the products.
SAP Module
	• SD
	• MM (Inventory involvement)
Main T-Code
	• VL01N – Create Outbound Delivery
	• (Display: VL03N, Change: VL02N)
What Happens in SAP?
	• Delivery document is created.
	• Stock availability is checked.
	• Warehouse starts picking and packing.
Business Example Warehouse prepares 100 motorcycles.
Database Impact
	• Delivery document created.
	• Inventory does not decrease yet.
SAP Basis Role
	• Monitor warehouse printing and system jobs.
	• Resolve user or printer issues.
SAP Cloud/DevOps Role
	• Ensure warehouse devices and SAP servers stay connected.
	• Monitor infrastructure health.

Step 3: Goods Issue (Most Important Step)
Goal
	• Officially ship the goods.
Business Activity Products leave the warehouse.
SAP Module
	• SD
	• MM
	• FI
Main T-Code
	• VL02N – Post Goods Issue (PGI)
Movement Type
	• 601
What Happens in SAP?
	• Inventory decreases.
	• Stock is removed.
	• Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) is posted.
	• Material document is created.
	• Accounting document is created.
Business Example The truck leaves the factory carrying 100 motorcycles.
Database Impact
	• Inventory decreases.
	• Financial records are updated.
SAP Basis Role
	• Monitor performance during high-volume shipping.
	• Ensure interfaces and work processes are healthy.
SAP Cloud/DevOps Role
	• Keep database and application servers available.
	• Monitor storage, compute, and network performance.
	Interview Question: At which step does inventory decrease?
	Answer: During Goods Issue (VL02N) with Movement Type 601.

Step 4: Billing
Goal
	• Create the customer invoice.
Business Activity Company requests payment from the customer.
SAP Module
	• SD
	• FI
Main T-Code
	• VF01 – Create Billing Document
	• (Display: VF03)
What Happens in SAP?
	• Invoice is generated.
	• Revenue is recognized.
	• Accounts Receivable (AR) is created.
Business Example Honda sends an invoice for the 100 motorcycles.
Database Impact
	• Billing document created.
	• Financial documents updated.
SAP Basis Role
	• Ensure billing jobs complete successfully.
	• Troubleshoot printing or spool issues.
SAP Cloud/DevOps Role
	• Monitor servers during month-end billing peaks.
	• Scale infrastructure if required.

Step 5: Payment Receipt
Goal
	• Receive payment from the customer.
Business Activity Customer pays the invoice.
SAP Module
	• FI (Financial Accounting)
Main T-Code
	• F-28 – Post Incoming Payment
What Happens in SAP?
	• Payment is recorded.
	• Bank account balance increases.
	• Accounts Receivable (AR) is cleared.
Business Example The Honda dealer transfers payment for the motorcycles.
Database Impact
	• Payment document created.
	• Customer balance updated.
	• Invoice marked as paid.
SAP Basis Role
	• Ensure financial transactions run reliably.
	• Monitor system availability and security.
SAP Cloud/DevOps Role
	• Protect financial systems with backups and disaster recovery.
	• Monitor infrastructure and automate operational tasks.

SAP Modules Used
Step	Business Activity	Module(s)	Main T-Code	Business Goal
1	Sales Order	SD	VA01	Record customer order
2	Delivery	SD, MM	VL01N	Prepare shipment

Key Points for Interviews
	• O2C stands for Order-to-Cash.
	• It manages the complete sales lifecycle, from customer order to payment.
	• Main modules: SD, MM, and FI.
	• Inventory decreases only at Goods Issue (VL02N, Movement Type 601).
	• Billing (VF01) creates the invoice and posts Accounts Receivable.
	• Payment Receipt (F-28) clears the customer's outstanding balance.
How this helps your SAP Basis ? SAP Cloud ? DevOps journey
Understanding O2C allows you to identify the business impact of technical issues:
	• If VA01 fails, sales orders cannot be created.
	• If VL02N fails, shipments stop.
	• If VF01 fails, invoices are not generated and revenue collection is delayed.
	• If F-28 or related financial processing fails, payments cannot be properly recorded.
A Basis, Cloud, or DevOps engineer may not perform these business transactions, but they are responsible for keeping the SAP landscape, databases, integrations, and infrastructure healthy so these critical business processes run continuously.
