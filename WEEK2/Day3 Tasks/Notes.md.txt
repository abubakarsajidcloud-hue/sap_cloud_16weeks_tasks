Topic: Fraud Prevention in SAP Procure-to-Pay (P2P)
SECTION 1 — CAREER CONTEXT
Why are you learning this?
Almost every company using SAP buys materials worth millions of dollars every year. Without controls, employees could:
	• Pay fake invoices
	• Pay twice for the same goods
	• Pay for goods never delivered
	• Overpay suppliers
The 3-Way Match is one of SAP's strongest financial controls to prevent these problems.
This topic is asked frequently in SAP interviews because it connects:
	• Business processes
	• SAP MM
	• SAP FI
	• Internal controls
	• Auditing
	• Fraud prevention
Why companies invest in it
	• Prevent financial fraud
	• Improve audit compliance
	• Reduce invoice errors
	• Protect company cash flow
Why a SAP Basis Engineer should know it
You won't configure the business rules, but you support the SAP system that enforces them. If invoice postings fail, users often contact the Basis team first. You need to know whether it's a business issue or a system issue.
Why a SAP Cloud Engineer should know it
When migrating SAP to the cloud, these business controls must continue working correctly. Cloud engineers monitor application availability, database health, and integrations so invoice processing is not interrupted.

SECTION 3 — MULTI-LEVEL EXPLANATION
Level 1 (Beginner)
Imagine you order a laptop online.
Before paying, you check:
	1. Did I order it?
	2. Did I receive it?
	3. Is the bill correct?
If all three match, you pay.
That is the 3-Way Match.

Level 2 (Student)
SAP compares:
	• Purchase Order (PO)
	• Goods Receipt (GR)
	• Invoice (MIRO)
If everything matches within allowed tolerance, SAP posts the invoice.
Otherwise, it blocks the invoice.

SECTION 6 — CORE CONCEPTS
Purchase Order (PO)
Definition: Agreement to buy goods.
Business Problem Solved: Defines what the company agreed to purchase.
Analogy: A signed shopping list.

Goods Receipt (GR)
Definition: Confirmation that goods actually arrived.
Business Problem Solved: Prevents paying before receiving goods.
Analogy: Signing that your courier delivered the package.

Invoice
Definition: Supplier's payment request.
Business Problem Solved: Creates the financial obligation.
Analogy: Restaurant bill after your meal.

3-Way Match
Definition: Comparison of PO, GR, and Invoice.
Business Problem Solved: Stops fraudulent or incorrect payments.
Cloud Example: Even when SAP runs on AWS, the application still performs the same business validation before posting invoices.
Interview Answer: "The 3-Way Match compares the purchase order, goods receipt, and vendor invoice. SAP only posts the invoice when these documents match within configured tolerances, helping prevent fraud and payment errors."

€2.3 Million Fraud Prevention Example
A manufacturing company issues a PO for €2.3 million worth of industrial machines.
A dishonest employee colludes with a supplier and submits an invoice for the full amount before any machines are delivered.
During MIRO, SAP performs the 3-way match:
	• PO: Exists for €2.3 million.
	• Goods Receipt: No delivery has been posted in MIGO.
	• Invoice: Requests payment.
Because there is no matching Goods Receipt, SAP blocks the invoice instead of posting it for payment. If price or quantity differences exceed the tolerance configured in OMR6 (for example, a 2% price tolerance), the invoice is also blocked.
The blocked invoice appears in MRBR, where the purchasing team investigates. They discover that no equipment has arrived, reject the invoice, and prevent a €2.3 million fraudulent payment.
This demonstrates why the 3-way match is one of the most important internal financial controls in SAP. It ensures that a company pays only for goods that were actually ordered, received, and correctly invoiced.

Troubleshooting Workshop
Scenario 1: Invoice blocked
	• Symptoms: MIRO displays invoice block.
	• Investigation: Compare PO, GR, and invoice.
	• Root Cause: Goods Receipt missing.
	• Resolution: Post the GR or correct the invoice.
	• Prevention: Ensure warehouse posts GR promptly.
Scenario 2: Price mismatch
	• Symptoms: Invoice blocked for variance.
	• Investigation: Compare PO price with invoice.
	• Root Cause: Supplier charged more than agreed.
	• Resolution: Update PO (if approved) or request a corrected invoice.
	• Prevention: Maintain accurate purchasing data.
Scenario 3: Quantity mismatch
	• Symptoms: Invoice quantity exceeds received quantity.
	• Investigation: Review MIGO and invoice.
	• Root Cause: Partial delivery or incorrect invoice.
	• Resolution: Wait for remaining delivery or correct the invoice.
	• Prevention: Coordinate warehouse and purchasing activities.

It answers:

Without a PO, anyone could submit an invoice and claim payment.

Duplicate Invoice Fraud
Sometimes a supplier (or an attacker) sends the same invoice twice hoping to be paid twice.
Example:
Invoice No.
INV-1001
Paid once.
Later, the same invoice number is submitted again.
SAP checks the invoice number, vendor, amount, and other details.
If duplicate invoice checking is enabled, SAP rejects or blocks the second invoice, preventing duplicate payment.

Tolerance Limits (OMR6)
Companies define acceptable differences.
Example:
Difference	Result
1%	Invoice accepted
2%	Invoice accepted (if within company policy)
5%	Invoice blocked
20%	Invoice blocked immediately
These tolerance limits allow small real-world differences (such as minor rounding or shipping adjustments) while preventing significant errors or fraud.

What Does a SAP Basis Engineer Do Here?
Although Basis engineers do not approve invoices, they support the technical platform:
	• Ensure MIRO, MIGO, and ME21N are available.
	• Monitor SAP application servers and databases.
	• Check work processes if postings are slow.
	• Analyze dumps or logs for technical failures.
	• Support users with authorization issues.

What Does a SAP Cloud/DevOps Engineer Do?
Cloud/DevOps engineers keep the infrastructure reliable:
	• Maintain SAP application servers on cloud VMs.
	• Monitor CPU, memory, disk, and network.
	• Ensure databases remain available.
	• Configure backups and disaster recovery.
	• Automate deployments and system monitoring.
	• Keep integrations with vendor systems secure and available.
